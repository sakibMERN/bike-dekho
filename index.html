Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if it's over 5 years old, has visible damage, worn straps, or no longer fits well. Also, if it's been in an accident or doesn't meet current safety standards, get a new one to ensure proper protection.

How could I choose a perfect bike for me?

To choose the perfect bike, consider your riding style (e.g., road, mountain, commuting), ensure the frame size fits your height, check the bike's weight and components, and set a budget. Test ride a few models to find one that's comfortable and suits your needs.

How many times you need to provide all the bike's paper?

You'll need to provide all the bike's papers when registering it, selling it, or making an insurance claim. Keep these documents safe for such occasions.
